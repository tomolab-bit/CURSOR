## Submit foreign loan repayment application on time to limit errors.

Late submission of documents, failure to comply with regulations on the deadline for submission of documents is the most common violation, always accounting for more than 50% of violations arising in relation to foreign loan repayment activities. Therefore, understanding the regulations on foreign loan repayment registration, specific regulations on documents and deadline for submitting loan documents... is necessary for businesses.

It can be said that the common violations that businesses often encounter in the process of registering for confirmation of foreign debt repayment with the State Bank's provincial and municipal branches are late submission of documents and failure to comply with regulations on the deadline for submitting documents, which is the most common violation, always accounting for over 50% of violations related to foreign debt repayment activities.

Submitting complete, correct and timely documents is the responsibility of the enterprise. However, from a management perspective, to help enterprises limit violations in foreign loan registration activities in general and in submitting documents on time in particular, relevant units need to pay attention to supporting enterprises and providing full consulting information.

In order to help businesses understand the regulations on foreign loan registration, specific regulations on documents and deadlines for submitting loan applications. With that meaning, following the article " *[Foreign loans must be registered with the State Bank of Vietnam's branches in provinces and cities](https://thitruongtaichinhtiente.vn/doanh-nghiep-can-quan-tam-han-che-vi-pham-trong-vay-tra-no-nuoc-ngoai-46590.html) "*, this article provides information on the regulations on the deadlines for submitting applications for confirmation of foreign loan registration. With the following specific cases:

***For medium and long-term loans:*** Enterprises need to submit documents within 30 working days from the date of signing the medium and long-term foreign loan agreement;

***For short-term to medium- and long-term loans:*** The deadline for submitting documents is in accordance with regulations, specifically in the following cases:

*For short-term loans with an extension agreement signed within 1 year from the date of first withdrawal*: Enterprises need to submit documents within 30 working days from the date of signing the agreement to extend the short-term foreign loan to medium or long-term.

*For short-term loans with a renewal agreement signed after 1 year from the date of first withdrawal*: Enterprises need to submit documents within 60 working days from the 1st anniversary date from the date of first withdrawal.

*For short-term loans without an extension agreement but with outstanding principal* *(including principal-backed interest) at the 1st anniversary of the first withdrawal date*: Enterprises must submit their application within 60 working days from the 1st anniversary of the first withdrawal date.

***In case of registration of loan changes:*** When there is any change in any content related to the loan stated in the registration confirmation document, the registration confirmation document of foreign loan changes of the State Bank, the enterprise must register the change of the foreign loan (except in the following cases: Change of capital withdrawal time, principal repayment within 10 working days compared to the plan confirmed by the State Bank; Change of the borrower's address but not changing the province or city where the borrower has its head office; Change of the lender, relevant information about the lender in the syndicated loan; Change of the commercial transaction name of the bank providing account services, the bank serving the secured transaction; Change of the interest and fee payment plan of the foreign loan compared to the plan confirmed by the State Bank in the registration confirmation document, the registration confirmation of foreign loan changes but not changing the method of determining interest and fees stipulated in the foreign loan agreement; Change (increase or (reduce) the amount of capital withdrawal, principal repayment, interest, and fees within 100 currency units of the foreign loan currency compared with the amount stated in the confirmation document; Change the actual amount of capital withdrawal, principal repayment of a specific period to be less than the amount stated in the capital withdrawal and repayment plan on the confirmation document).

*Accordingly, the deadline for submitting loan change registration application is as follows:*

\- Enterprises must register loan changes within 30 working days from the date of signing the change agreement or before the date of implementing the change content.

\- Enterprises shall register changes to loans within 30 working days from the date the organization inheriting the obligation to repay the foreign loan is granted a business registration certificate or the date the parties sign an agreement on changing the borrower in case the original borrower is divided, separated, merged, or consolidated (whichever comes later) and before continuing to withdraw capital and repay the foreign loan.

\- Enterprises must register loan changes within 30 working days from the date the company completes updating information on name change and/or change of address to move head office to another province or city.

\- The enterprise shall register the change of the loan within 30 working days from the date the lender (or the organization representing the lender in the syndicated loan - if any), the guarantor, the guarantor or other related parties stated in the registration confirmation document, the change registration confirmation document sends a written notice to the borrower about the name change and before making any money transfer transactions related to these parties.

Above are some informational contents on regulations on the deadline for submitting registration dossiers for confirmation of foreign debt repayment loans not guaranteed by the Government according to Circular 12/2022/TT-NHNN. Enterprises borrowing and repaying foreign debt need to pay attention to grasp and implement well, ensuring this activity is in accordance with regulations, using loan capital effectively and safely, limiting violations.

*For any information or clarification, or specific instructions, businesses should contact the State Bank branch in the province or city for detailed advice and instructions.*
