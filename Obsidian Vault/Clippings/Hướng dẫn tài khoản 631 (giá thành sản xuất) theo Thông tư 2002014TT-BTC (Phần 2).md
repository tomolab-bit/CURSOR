### 1.8. Accounting principles for the transportation industry

Account 631 (Production cost) applied to the transportation industry must be accounted for in detail for each type of activity (passenger transport, freight transport, etc.). During the transportation process, tires wear out at a faster rate than the depreciation rate of the vehicle head, so they often have to be replaced many times, but the value of replacement tires is not included in the transportation cost at once when they are used for replacement, but must be deducted in advance or gradually allocated to production and business costs each period.

### 1.9. Accounting principles for hotel business operations

In hotel business, accounting for account 631 must be tracked in detail for each type of activity such as: Food and beverage activities, room service, entertainment services, other services (laundry, ironing, haircut, telegram, massage...).

## 2\. Structure and content of account 631 (Production cost)

Account 611 (Production cost) has the following structure and content:

**Debit:**

\- Cost of production and unfinished services at the beginning of the period.

\- Actual production and service costs incurred during the period.

**Creditor:**

\- Cost of products in stock and completed services is transferred to account 632 "Cost of goods sold".

\- Unfinished production and service costs at the end of the period are transferred to account 154 "Unfinished production and service costs".

**Account 631 has no ending balance.**

## 3\. Accounting method for some major economic transactions of account 631 (Production cost)

### 3.1. For production and business costs, unfinished service costs at the beginning of the period

Transfer production, business costs, and unfinished service costs at the beginning of the period to the Debit side of account 631 (Production cost), record:

Debit account 631 - Production cost.

Credit account 154 - Work in progress costs.

### 3.2. For raw material and material costs at the end of the accounting period

At the end of the accounting period, transfer the cost of raw materials and direct materials to the production cost account, record:

Debit account 631 - Production cost.

Account 621 - Direct material and raw material costs.

### 3.3. For labor costs at the end of the accounting period

At the end of the accounting period, transfer direct labor costs to the production cost account, record:

Debit account 631 - Production cost.

There is account 622 - Direct labor costs.

### 3.4. For the final general manufacturing costs

At the end of the period, calculate the allocation and transfer of general production costs to the production cost account for each type of product, labor, service, etc., record:

Debit account 631 - Production cost.

Debit account 632 - Cost of goods sold (unallocated fixed general manufacturing costs).

There is account 627 - General manufacturing costs.

### 3.5. For the value of unfinished products and services at the end of the period

At the end of the accounting period, conduct an inventory and determine the value of unfinished products and services at the end of the period, record;

Debit account 154 - Cost of production and business in progress.

Account 631 - Production cost.

### 3.6. For the cost of products in stock and completed services

Cost of products in stock, completed services, recorded:

Debit account 632 - Cost of goods sold.
