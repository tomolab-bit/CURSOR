## Resolution No. 57-NQ/TW sets the target for the ratio of enterprises with innovation activities to the total number of enterprises by 2030.

Pursuant to Sub-section 1, Section 2 [of Resolution 57-NQ/TW of 2024,](https://thuvienphapluat.vn/documents/law.aspx?id=E=pNM01qUTTm&mode=0=hWalh6STYl) the objectives are stipulated as follows:

> **II- OBJECTIVES**

> 1\. By 2030

> \- The potential, level of science, technology and innovation have reached an advanced level in many important fields, among the leading groups among upper-middle-income countries; the level, capacity of technology and innovation of enterprises have reached a level above the world average; a number of science and technology fields have reached international levels. Vietnam is among the top 3 countries in Southeast Asia, the top 50 countries in the world in terms of digital competitiveness and e-Government development index; the top 3 countries in Southeast Asia in terms of research and development of artificial intelligence, the center for developing a number of digital technology industries and fields in which Vietnam has advantages. There are at least 5 digital technology enterprises on par with advanced countries.

> \- The contribution of total factor productivity (TFP) to economic growth is over 55%; the proportion of high-tech product exports to the total value of exported goods reaches at least 50%. The scale of the digital economy reaches at least 30% of GDP. The rate of online public service use by people and businesses reaches over 80%; non-cash transactions reach 80%. The rate of enterprises with innovation activities reaches over 40% of the total number of enterprises. Science, technology and innovation make an important contribution to building and developing the cultural, social and human values of Vietnam, contributing to the human development index (HDI) maintaining above 0.7.

> \[...\]

Thus, the target by 2030 for the proportion of enterprises with innovation activities in the total number of enterprises is **40%.**

## What rights do businesses have?

Pursuant to Article 7 [of the Law on Enterprises 2020](https://thuvienphapluat.vn/documents/law.aspx?id=g=RJM016QXTk&mode=M=dsbGRWODWk), enterprises have the following rights:

\- Freedom to do business in industries and professions that are not prohibited by law.

\- Business autonomy and choice of business organization form; proactively choose business sector, occupation, location, and business form; proactively adjust business scale and sector.

\- Select the form and method of capital mobilization, allocation and use.

\- Freedom to search for markets, customers and sign contracts.

\- Export and import business.

\- Recruit, hire and use labor according to labor laws.

\- Proactively apply science and technology to improve business efficiency and competitiveness; intellectual property rights are protected according to intellectual property laws.

\- Possession, use and disposal of business assets.

\- Refuse requests from agencies, organizations and individuals to provide resources not in accordance with the provisions of law.

\- Complain and participate in litigation according to the provisions of law.

\- Other rights as prescribed by law.

## What criteria must social enterprises meet?

Pursuant to Article 10 [of the Law on Enterprises 2020,](https://thuvienphapluat.vn/documents/law.aspx?id=g=RJM016QXTk&mode=09dsbGRWOHhNQTWk) which stipulates the criteria, rights and obligations of social enterprises:

> **Article 10. Criteria, rights and obligations of social enterprises**

> 1\. Social enterprises must meet the following criteria:

> a) Is an enterprise registered in accordance with the provisions of this Law;

> b) Operational goals aim to solve social and environmental problems for the benefit of the community;

> c) Use at least 51% of the enterprise's total annual after-tax profit for reinvestment to achieve registered goals.

> 2\. In addition to the rights and obligations of enterprises as prescribed in this Law, social enterprises have the following rights and obligations:

> a) Owners and managers of social enterprises shall be considered, facilitated and supported in the issuance of relevant licenses, certificates and certifications in accordance with the provisions of law;

> b) Mobilize and receive funding from individuals, businesses, non-governmental organizations and other organizations in Vietnam and abroad to offset management and operating costs of the enterprise;

> \[...\]

According to the above regulations, social enterprises must meet the following criteria:

\- Is an enterprise registered and established according to regulations

\- Operational goals are to solve social and environmental problems for the benefit of the community.

\- Use at least 51% of the enterprise's total annual after-tax profit for reinvestment to achieve registered goals.
