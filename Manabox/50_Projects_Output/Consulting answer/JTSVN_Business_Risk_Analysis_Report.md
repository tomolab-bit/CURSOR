# JTSVN Business Risk Analysis Report (English)

## Executive Summary

### Current Situation
JTSVN is currently engaging in and generating revenue from businesses such as "video and photography production" and "printing services" that are not listed in its Investment Registration Certificate (IRC) and Enterprise Registration Certificate (ERC). This discrepancy between the actual business activities and the legally registered business scope is the primary issue identified in this report.

### Key Risks
This situation creates two significant risks:
1. **Legal Risk (High):** Administrative fines for failure to file notifications regarding changes in business scope. Combined violations of IRC and ERC could result in fines up to **130 million VND**.

### Detailed Legal Framework
- **Business Freedom and Registration Obligation (Enterprise Law):**
  The Enterprise Law generally recognizes that enterprises may freely conduct businesses not prohibited by law. However, this freedom is not unconditional; when business content is added or changed, there is an obligation to notify the authorities within 10 days. This "freedom" and "obligation" are inseparable.

- **Special Obligations for Foreign-Invested Enterprises (Investment Law):**
  In addition to the above obligations applicable to domestic enterprises, JTSVN, as an FDI enterprise, must also amend the IRC (Investment Registration Certificate) details in accordance with the Investment Law. Changes in business content are considered changes to investment plans, so adjustment procedures for both ERC (Enterprise Registration Certificate) and IRC are mandatory.

- **Penalties for Late Notification (Decree 122/2021/ND-CP):**
  If ERC change notifications are neglected, fines are imposed based on the number of days of delay.
  - **11-30 days delay:** 3,000,000-5,000,000 VND
  - **31-90 days delay:** 5,000,000-10,000,000 VND
  - **91 days or more delay:** 10,000,000-20,000,000 VND
  - **No notification:** 20,000,000-30,000,000 VND

- **Tax Treatment (Invoice and Deductible Expenses):**
  Even for activities in unregistered industries, invoice issuance is a legal obligation once transactions occur. Additionally, expenses incurred for such activities are generally recognized as deductible expenses in corporate tax calculations if they meet the normal deductibility requirements (business relevance, proper documentation, etc.).

### Practical Key Points

From the above official opinions, practical applications can be summarized as follows:

1. **Validity of Invoices:**
   Tax authorities have consistently stated that "VAT invoices issued for industries not registered by enterprises (provided they are not prohibited or restricted by law) do not constitute illegal invoices." This means that issued invoices are considered legally valid.

2. **Tax Treatment (VAT and CIT):**
   Since invoices are valid, buyer enterprises are allowed to use such invoices for input VAT deduction. As stated in the Hanoi Tax Department's opinion, expenses related to such transactions can also be included as deductible expenses in Corporate Income Tax (CIT) calculations.

3. **Separation of Tax Treatment and Administrative Penalties:**
   Importantly, tax treatment and administrative penalties under the Planning and Investment Law are treated as separate issues. In other words, **while the tax validity of transactions is recognized, administrative penalties (fines) for failure to change business registration content may be imposed separately**.

In conclusion, in practice, "issuing invoices promptly when transactions occur, and then quickly performing procedures to add or change business registration" is the key to achieving both legal compliance and business flexibility.
